What Are Digital Products?

Digital products are downloadable or deliverable files that can be sold an unlimited number of times with zero additional production cost. Examples include: e-books, PDF guides, Canva templates, Excel spreadsheets, design presets, Notion dashboards, online courses, stock photos, music, and software tools. The profit margin is essentially 100% after creation.

Step 1 – Create Your Product

E-book: Write in Google Docs, design in Canva, export as PDF. Aim for 20–60 pages.
Templates: Design in Canva, export shareable template link or PDF.
Course: Record lessons with Loom (free), host on Teachable or Udemy.

Step 2 – Choose Your Selling Platform

  • Etsy: Excellent built-in audience. $0.20/listing. 6.5% transaction fee.
  • Gumroad: Simple setup, 10% fee. Best for writers and creators.
  • Payhip: 5% fee. Good for courses and e-books.
  • Your Own Website: Zero platform fees. Requires more marketing effort.

Step 3 – Market Your Products

  • Create Pinterest boards showcasing your product benefits
  • Post on Instagram and TikTok showing your product in action
  • Guest post on related blogs with a link to your product
  • Build an email list and notify subscribers on launch
  • Run Etsy promoted listings for initial visibility

Pros

  • 100% profit margin after creation
  • Unlimited sales from one product
  • Complete location independence
  • No shipping, no inventory

Cons

  • Requires marketing to drive traffic
  • Piracy is a real risk
  • Platform algorithm changes

FAQ

Do I need technical skills to create digital products? +

No. Canva templates, e-books, and spreadsheets can all be created with free tools — no coding or design degree required. The most important ingredient is expertise or curation of valuable information.
