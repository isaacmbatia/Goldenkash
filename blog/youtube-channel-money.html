How to Start a YouTube Channel and Make Money in 2026

✍️
GoldenKash Editorial Team
March 2026 · 14 min read
⚠️ Affiliate Disclosure: Some links are affiliate links. We may earn a commission at no extra cost to you.

YouTube remains one of the most powerful income platforms in 2026. With over 2.7 billion monthly active users and multiple monetisation paths — AdSense, sponsorships, memberships, merchandising, and affiliate links — a successful YouTube channel can generate life-changing income. This is your complete, step-by-step guide.

💰 Realistic YouTube Income

0–3 months: $0 (growth phase)  |  3–12 months: $100–$1,500/mo  |  Year 1+: $1,000–$20,000+/mo depending on niche and audience size

Step 1: Choose Your Niche

Your niche determines your audience, monetisation potential, and CPM rate (cost per 1,000 views). Choose a niche you're knowledgeable and/or passionate about, but also research the monetisation potential.

Step 2: Set Up Your Channel

  1. Create a Google Account → go to youtube.com → click your profile → Create Channel
  2. Choose a channel name — easy to spell, memorable, and niche-relevant
  3. Design channel art — use Canva to create a professional banner and icon
  4. Write your About section — include keywords naturally
  5. Set up your links — Instagram, website, email for sponsorships

Step 3: Equipment (Start Simple)

You don't need expensive gear to start. Many successful channels began with just a smartphone.

  • Camera: Your smartphone camera is fine to start. Upgrade later.
  • Microphone: A lapel mic ($15–$30 on Amazon) makes a huge difference
  • Lighting: Natural window light or a $20 ring light
  • Editing: CapCut (free, mobile) or DaVinci Resolve (free, desktop)

Step 4: Create Optimised Content

Each video should serve one specific audience intent. Structure every video with a strong hook (first 30 seconds), clear value delivery, and a call-to-action to like, subscribe, or visit your link.

  • Research keywords using TubeBuddy or VidIQ (both have free tiers)
  • Write SEO-optimised titles: "How to [Result] [Timeframe]"
  • Design click-worthy thumbnails — bright, high-contrast, clear text
  • Use chapters (timestamps) to improve watch time
  • Post consistently: 1–2 times per week minimum

Step 5: Monetisation Paths

  • 🎯 YouTube Partner Program — 1,000 subscribers + 4,000 watch hours. AdSense pays $1–$30 per 1,000 views
  • 🔗 Affiliate Marketing — Include links in descriptions. Earn 3–50% commission
  • 🤝 Brand Sponsorships — Start at 5,000–10,000 subscribers. $50–$5,000 per sponsored integration
  • 🛍️ Merchandise — Printful or Printify integration. Sell branded products to fans
  • 🎓 Digital Products — Sell courses, ebooks, or presets directly to your audience

🛠️ Recommended YouTube Tools

  • TubeBuddy — keyword research and A/B thumbnail testing
  • Canva — thumbnail and banner design
  • CapCut or DaVinci Resolve — free video editing
  • VidIQ — channel analytics and growth strategies
  • Social Blade — competitive channel research

✅ Pros

  • Multiple income streams from one channel
  • Content works 24/7 for you
  • Build a loyal community
  • Audiences globally accessible
  • Low startup cost

❌ Cons

  • Takes 6–12 months to reach monetisation threshold
  • Algorithm changes can hurt channels
  • Requires consistent effort
  • Video production takes time

FAQ

How long does it take to monetise a YouTube channel? +

Most channels reach the YouTube Partner Program threshold (1,000 subs + 4,000 watch hours) in 6–18 months with consistent weekly uploads. Faster growth is possible with viral content or strong SEO.

Can I start a YouTube channel if I'm camera shy? +

Absolutely. Faceless YouTube channels (screen recordings, animations, AI voiceovers) are very successful. See our dedicated guide on making money on YouTube without showing your face.

How much money does 1 million views earn on YouTube? +

On average, 1 million views earns $1,000–$10,000 depending on your niche and audience location. Finance and tech niches earn the most; entertainment and gaming earn the least per view.
