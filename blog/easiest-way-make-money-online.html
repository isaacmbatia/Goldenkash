What’s the Easiest Way to Make Money Online in 2026?

🌍 Geographic Note: While these strategies work globally, this guide is highly optimized for readers in the United States, United Kingdom, Canada, and Australia looking to earn in USD/GBP.
✍️
GoldenKash Editorial Team
March 2026 · 7 min read

When people search for the "easiest" way to make money online, what they usually mean is: "How can I make money right now, without needing a degree, without building a website, and without investing upfront capital?"

If that is your goal, you need to focus on low-barrier-to-entry micro-tasks and gig economy platforms. Let's break down the absolute easiest methods currently working.

1. Microtasking and Surveys

This is the definition of "easy," though it won't make you rich. Platforms like Swagbucks, InboxDollars, and Prolific pay you for your opinion.

  • Prolific.com: This is the gold standard for surveys. Researchers from universities (like Oxford and Stanford) pay you to participate in behavioral studies. It is strictly vetted and pays a fair hourly wage (around $8 to $12 an hour).
  • Clickworker / Remotasks: You get paid to do tiny, repetitive tasks like categorizing images, transcribing audio snippets, and training AI models.

2. Testing Websites and Apps

Companies need to know if their new website is confusing to average users. They pay platforms like UserTesting.com or TryMyUI to recruit normal people to click around their prototypes.

You download a screen recorder, speak your thoughts out loud as you navigate their mock website ("I can't find the checkout button... oh, there it is"), and you get paid $10 for a 20-minute test. If you qualify for live interviews, they pay $60 for an hour on Zoom.

3. Selling Unused Bandwidth

This is the literal easiest method because it requires zero active work. Apps like Honeygain or Pawns.app run in the background of your computer or phone. They securely rent out your unused internet bandwidth to data scientists and researchers.

You install it, let it run, and it slowly accumulates money. You might only make $15 to $30 a month, but it requires zero effort beyond the initial installation.

The Catch

The easier the task, the lower the pay. While surveys and testing are great for making your first $100 online, they are not scalable. Once you prove to yourself that making money online is real, you should graduate to harder, but vastly more profitable methods like affiliate marketing or freelance copywriting.
