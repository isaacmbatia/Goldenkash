The Two Models Explained

Amazon FBA: You source or create products, ship inventory to Amazon's warehouse, and Amazon handles storage, packing, and delivery. You build a brand. Dropshipping: You list products on a store (Shopify/WooCommerce); when a customer orders, your supplier ships directly to them. Zero inventory is held by you.

Amazon FBA — Deeper Analysis

FBA gives you access to 300M+ active buyers with Prime shipping. Success requires careful product research, quality control, and understanding Amazon's complex fee structure. Amazon takes 15-40% in fees, but your net margins can reach 25-40% with optimised products. FBA is better for building a long-term asset you can eventually sell (typical Amazon brand valuations: 3-4× annual profit).

Dropshipping — Deeper Analysis

Dropshipping carries zero inventory risk but requires you to build and drive traffic to your own store — typically via Facebook/Instagram/TikTok ads or SEO. Margins are lower (10-25%) but startup costs are minimal. Shopify + DSers integration makes the technical side straightforward. Success hinges on your ability to find winning products fast and run profitable ad campaigns — a skill with a real learning curve.

Which Should You Choose?

  • Choose Dropshipping if: you have under to invest, want to learn advertising, and want to validate ideas quickly.
  • Choose Amazon FBA if: you have ,000+ to invest, want to build a real brand, and prefer leveraging Amazon's existing buyer trust.
  • Consider both: Many successful e-commerce entrepreneurs run dropshipping stores to identify winning products, then manufacture those products for Amazon FBA once validated.

FBA Best For

  • Brand building
  • Consistent passive income
  • Higher trust with buyers
  • Sellable business asset

Dropshipping Best For

  • Zero inventory risk
  • Low startup cost
  • Fast product testing
  • Location independence
